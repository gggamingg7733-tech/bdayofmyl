The Little Kite

a tiny story about flying ✦
one

This is Pip — a small paper kite with one very big dream: to touch the sky.

two

But every time Pip leapt for the clouds… it tumbled right back down. Again. And again.

three

“Maybe I’m just not built for the sky,” Pip sighed, lying flat in the grass.

four

Then the sky turned dark. Thunder. Wind. The biggest storm anyone had ever seen. Everyone ran and hid.

five

The wind grabbed Pip and hurled it upward — spinning, shaking, terrifying. But Pip held on tight.

six

And then something strange happened. The harder the storm pushed… the higher Pip climbed.

seven

Up, up, past the clouds — into the gold. The very storm that scared everyone had carried Pip higher than any kite before. “The wind was never against me,” Pip whispered. “It was lifting me all along.”

Hard winds don’t break the brave.
They raise them.

and you know what…

Stories like that one — they remind me of ours.

our first gusts

When we started, we didn’t really know how to fly either. We caught a little wind, we dropped, we tried again — figuring each other out one gust at a time.

learning to fly

And slowly we found our rhythm. The easy laughter, the small ordinary magic, the days the wind was kind and we just… soared together.

our storms

We’ve had our storms too. The arguments, the distance, the days it felt like we were tumbling back toward the ground. But every single time — we held on. We never let go of the string.

and right now…

Right now is just another gust — a heavy one, I know. But look how far we’ve already climbed. We don’t crash the way we used to. We rise.
